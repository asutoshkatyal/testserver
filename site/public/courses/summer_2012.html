Philosophy 1 | Introduction to Philosophy | NH 1105 | Session B (MTWR 9:30am-10:25am) + Discussion
 
 
 

This course will introduce students to some of the fundamental topics and methods of contemporary Western philosophy. The course will focus on issues regarding the existence of God, free will, personal identity, and material constitution.

We will address questions such as: Can we prove that God exists? Is the existence of God compatible with the existence of evil in the world? Are we identical with immaterial souls, or are we wholly material things? What are the conditions such that a person continues to exist through time? Do we have free will? Is free will compatible with determinism? Do ordinary objects such as ships, statues and baseballs exist? Can two material objects be located in the same place at the same time?

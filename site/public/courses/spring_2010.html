Philosophy 130
|
 Freedom and Determinism
| GIRV 2119
(TR 9:30am-10:45am)

Do we really have free will? The universe (or at least many parts of it) seems deterministic in that universal laws seem to make only one future state of affairs possible.  But is this compatible with our ever acting freely or being morally responsible for what we do?  Are our actions ever ultimately up to us?  Do we really have the kind of control over our actions that we normally think we do? In this class, we’ll consider contemporary philosophical arguments for certain answers to these questions.  If time permits, we’ll also examine some recent empirical work in psychology, neuroscience, and so-called “experimental philosophy” that bears on these issues.

Course web page: http://www.joshdmay.com/teaching/course1/
